Fire Damage Restoration Process

If you are here, we assume that you have already made Emergency Contact to extinguish the fire, if you have not stop reading and do so!

  1. Safety is first and foremost. Remove yourself from the property until the fire is out and professionals have evaluated whether it is safe or not to return.
  2. Inspection and Assessment: Once the fire has been extinguished, before you actually begin moving things or cleaning, your insurance assessors will need to take photographs to evaluate the loss due to the fire for which you can claim compensation. Lots of pictures!
    1. Insurance Company: Allow your adjuster to visit the property and prepare their estimate for your claim.
    2. Restoration Company: Around the same time a restoration company can also prepare your estimate to negotiate on your behalf vs. the insurance providers estimate if necessary.
  4. Board Up / Tarp Service: Water Mold Fire is able to board up any broken windows and tarp a damaged roof so further damage does not occur.
  5. Pack Out and Storage: We will pack out your personal belongings and place them in storage while work is being done. Each item is documented to ensure its safe return.
  6. Cleanup: The sooner you get started on this the better. Fire damaged building material will not get any worse but the longer items damaged by smoke and water go without attention, the worse it gets. Only a qualified Restoration company like Water Mold Fire should be performing the work below.
    1. Water Removal- If water was used to put out the fire, this must be extracted and drying equipment put in place to dry the property.
    2. Demolition- Any fire damaged building materials need to be removed and thrown out. Materials that are within close proximity showing Smoke and Soot damage may also have to be removed. Depending on the severity of the fire, you may also need duct work replaced.
    3. Cleaning – A thorough cleaning with smoke / odor removal products must be used at the entire property to make sure there are no lingering issues present.
  8. Reconstruction: After the cleaning is complete and there are no signs of fire damaged materials or smoke odors, the rebuilding process can begin.

Fire Damage Prevention

Once the fire has been put out and the Fire Damage Repairs have been performed, it is time to start thinking about prevention in the future. Here are some simple tips that can be used to stop more fires or at least limit the amount of damage they do.

Fire Extinguishers

Installing fire extinguishers in your home can help to fight the fire before help arrives and this can also help to reduce the damage caused by the fire.

Sprinkler System

Installing fire sprinklers in your home can help to reduce the destruction due to a fire by around 90%, which can in turn help to reduce your insurance premiums. Sprinklers help to prevent the spreading of fire to other parts of your home by spraying water on the fire. Also, sprinklers use lesser water, almost 8.5 times lesser than traditional fire hoses while putting out a fire. Unfortunately this is very difficult unless the property is new construction.

Smoke Detectors

These are extremely useful and trigger an alarm to alert people in case of a fire outbreak. There are 2 types of smoke detectors – ionization smoke detectors and photoelectric smoke detectors. There are also smoke detectors that can be part of a SMART home and notify you via a mobile app even when you are not at home.

No Open Flames

Limit or eliminate the amount of open flames you have in your home or business. This includes candles, cigaretts, matches, lighters, etc. If you don’t use fire in your house, it drastically reduces your chance of getting fire damage!

Final Tips for Fire Damage Restoration

When a fire occurs, the period between 24-48 hours after the fire is known as the “golden hours” within which fire damage restoration can be done. This is the ideal period to start the fire damage restoration process. Here are some tips to get you started:

  • Do NOT enter the home until it has been deemed safe and structurally sound by a qualified professional.
  • Beginning the damage recovery process early can help to prevent the costs of restoration from rising due to stains forming on furnishings, carpets and other materials.
  • However, it is not advisable to start the restoration process until 24 hours after the fire, as during this period all the smoke and dust will settle, which can cause respiratory problems.
  • The process should also not be started until documentation by yourself, a representative of yourself and your insurance company, so this can often delay the process.
  • When returning to your home after a fire outbreak, it is advisable to wear masks to avoid breathing in the smoke and dust, which can be harmful.
  • Avoid attempting to clean the draperies, upholstery of the furniture, carpets and other fabrics, as this will only cause the soot to get smeared further and cause greater damage.
